Continous-time State-space Model

 

$$ \begin{aligned} \dot{x}&=Ax+Bu\\ y&=Hx \end{aligned} $$ with $x\in\mathbb{R}^n$, $u\in\mathbb{R}^m$, $y\in\mathbb{R}^p$.

In order to have a well-defined $\mathcal{H}_2$ norm, the system cannot posses the transmission term $D$ of $u$ to $y$.

MIMO Transfer Function

$$ G(s)=H(sI-A)^{-1}B=\begin{bmatrix} G_{11}(s) & \cdots & G_{1m}(s)\\ G_{21}(s) & \cdots & G_{2m}(s)\\ \vdots & \ddots & \vdots\\ G_{p1}(s) & \cdots & G_{pm}(s) \end{bmatrix} $$

such that $G_{ij}(s)$ is the transfer function from the $j$-th input to the $i$-th output.

$\mathcal{H}_2$ norm (Frequency domain)

$$ \|G\|_2=\frac{1}{2\pi}\sqrt{\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \text{tr}(G(j\omega)^*G(j\omega))d\omega} $$

For a SISO system, the $\mathcal{H}_2$ norm is given by: $$ \|G\|_2=\frac{1}{2\pi}\sqrt{\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} |G(j\omega)|^2d\omega} $$

$\mathcal{H}_2$ norm (Time domain)

From Parseval's Theorem:

$$ \|G\|_2=\sqrt{\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \text{tr}(g(t)^\trp g(t))dt} $$

For a SISO system, the $\mathcal{H}_2$ norm is given by: $$ \|G\|_2=\sqrt{\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} g(t)^2dt} $$

$\mathcal{H}_2$ norm (Time domain)

From Parseval's Theorem:

$$ \|G\|_2=\sqrt{\int_{0}^{\infty} \text{tr}(g(t)^\trp g(t))dt} $$

For a SISO system, the $\mathcal{H}_2$ norm is given by: $$ \|G\|_2=\sqrt{\int_{0}^{\infty} |g(t)|^2dt} $$

$\mathcal{H}_2$ norm (Time domain)

 

$\mathcal{H}_2$ norm (Time domain) in SS

 

$$ \|G\|_2^2=\int_{0}^{\infty} \text{tr}(g(t)^\trp g(t))dt $$

with $g(t)=\mathcal{L}^{-1}\{G(s)\}$ is the impulse response of the system.

$\mathcal{H}_2$ norm (Time domain) in SS

 

From SS representation, we have:

$$ g(t)=\begin{cases} He^{At}B, & \text{if } t\geq 0\\ 0, & \text{if } t<0 \end{cases} $$

$\mathcal{H}_2$ norm (Time domain) in SS

 

$$ \|G\|_2^2=\int_{0}^{\infty} \text{tr}(B^\trp \underbrace{e^{A^\trp t}H^\trp H e^{At}}_{W_o} B) dt\\ =\int_{0}^{\infty} \text{tr}( H \underbrace{e^{At} B B^\trp e^{A^\trp t}}_{W_c}H^\trp ) dt $$

Gramians of Observability and Controlability

Controllability Gramian: $$ W_c=\int_0^\infty e^{At} B B^\trp e^{A^\trp t} dt $$

Observability Gramian: $$ W_o=\int_0^\infty e^{A^\trp t} H^\trp H e^{A t} dt $$

Lyapunov Matrix Equations

Controllability Gramian: $$ AW_c+W_cA^\trp+BB^\trp=0 $$

Observability Gramian: $$ A^\trp W_o+W_oA+H^\trp H=0 $$

$\mathcal{H}_2$ norm (Time domain) in SS

$$ \|G\|_2^2=\text{tr}(B^\trp W_o B)=\text{tr}(HW_cH^\trp) $$ such that

$$ A^\trp W_o+W_oA+H^\trp H=0 $$

or

$$ AW_c+W_cA^\trp+BB^\trp=0 $$

$\mathcal{H}_2$ norm via LMI (Case 1)

$$ \|G\|_2^2=\text{tr}(B^\trp P B) $$ such that

$$ \min_{P=P^\trp \succ 0} \text{tr}(B^\trp P B) \\ \text{s. t.} \\ AP+PA^\trp+BB^\trp\preceq 0 $$

$\mathcal{H}_2$ norm via LMI (Case 2)

$$ \|G\|_2^2=\text{tr}(H P H^\trp ) $$ such that

$$ \min_{P=P^\trp \succ 0} \text{tr}(H P H^\trp ) \\ \text{s. t.} \\ A^\trp P+PA+H^\trp H\preceq 0 $$

Discrete-time State-space Model

 

$$ \begin{aligned} x_{k+1}&=Ax_k+Bu_k\\ y_k&=Hx_k+Du_k \end{aligned} $$ with $x\in\mathbb{R}^n$, $u\in\mathbb{R}^m$, $y\in\mathbb{R}^p$.

In discrete-time, the system can posses the transmission term $D$! However, we need to account for $\tr[D^\trp D]$ in the $\mathcal{H}_2$ norm.

MIMO Transfer Function

$$ G(z)=H(zI-A)^{-1}B=\begin{bmatrix} G_{11}(z) & \cdots & G_{1m}(z)\\ G_{21}(zs) & \cdots & G_{2m}(z)\\ \vdots & \ddots & \vdots\\ G_{p1}(z) & \cdots & G_{pm}(z) \end{bmatrix} $$

such that $G_{ij}(z)$ is the transfer function from the $j$-th input to the $i$-th output.

$\mathcal{H}_2$ norm (Frequency domain)

$$ \|G\|_2=\frac{1}{2\pi}\sqrt{\int_{-\pi}^{\pi} \text{tr}(G(e^{j\omega})^*G(e^{j\omega}))d\omega} $$

For a SISO system, the $\mathcal{H}_2$ norm is given by: $$ \|G\|_2=\frac{1}{2\pi}\sqrt{\int_{-\pi}^{\pi} |G(e^{j\omega})|^2d\omega} $$

$\mathcal{H}_2$ norm (Time domain)

From Parseval's Theorem:

$$ \|G\|_2=\sqrt{\sum_{k=-\infty}^{\infty} \text{tr}(g_k^\trp g_k)} $$

For a SISO system, the $\mathcal{H}_2$ norm is given by: $$ \|G\|_2=\sqrt{\sum_{k=-\infty}^{\infty} g_k^2} $$

$\mathcal{H}_2$ norm (Time domain) in SS

 

From SS representation, we have:

$$ g_k=\begin{cases} HA^{k-1}B, & \text{if } k> 0\\ 0, & \text{if } k\leq 0 \end{cases} $$

$\mathcal{H}_2$ norm (Time domain)

From Parseval's Theorem:

$$ \|G\|_2=\sqrt{\sum_{k=1}^{\infty} \text{tr}(g_k^\trp g_k)} $$

For a SISO system, the $\mathcal{H}_2$ norm is given by: $$ \|G\|_2=\sqrt{\sum_{k=1}^{\infty} g_k^2} $$

$\mathcal{H}_2$ norm (Time domain)

$\mathcal{H}_2$ norm (Time domain) in SS

 

$$ \|G\|_2^2=\sum_{k=1}^{\infty} \text{tr}(B^\trp \underbrace{(A^{k-1})^\trp H^\trp H A^{k-1}}_{W_o} B)\\ =\sum_{k=1}^{\infty} \text{tr}( H \underbrace{A^{k-1} B B^\trp (A^{k-1})^\trp }_{W_c}H^\trp ) $$

Gramians of Observability and Controlability

Controllability Gramian: $$ W_c=\sum_{k=1}^\infty A^{k-1} B B^\trp (A^{k-1})^\trp $$

Observability Gramian: $$ W_o=\sum_{k=1}^\infty (A^{k-1})^\trp H^\trp H A^{k-1} $$

Lyapunov Matrix Equations

Controllability Gramian: $$ AW_cA^\trp -W_c+BB^\trp=0 $$

Observability Gramian: $$ A^\trp W_o A-W_o+H^\trp H=0 $$

$\mathcal{H}_2$ norm (discrete-time)

$$ \|G\|_2^2=\text{tr}(B^\trp W_o B)=\text{tr}(HW_cH^\trp) $$ such that

$$ A^\trp W_o A-W_o+H^\trp H=0 $$

or

$$ AW_cA^\trp -W_c+BB^\trp=0 $$

$\mathcal{H}_2$ norm via LMI (Case 1)

$$ \|G\|_2^2=\text{tr}(B^\trp P B) $$ such that

$$ \min_{P=P^\trp \succ 0} \text{tr}(B^\trp P B) \\ \text{s. t.} \\ APA^\trp-P+BB^\trp\preceq 0 $$

$\mathcal{H}_2$ norm via LMI (Case 2)

$$ \|G\|_2^2=\text{tr}(H P H^\trp ) $$ such that

$$ \min_{P=P^\trp \succ 0} \text{tr}(H P H^\trp ) \\ \text{s. t.} \\ A^\trp PA-P+H^\trp H\preceq 0 $$

$\mathcal{H}_2$ norm: examples

 

Examples in Matlab

Optimal $\mathcal{H}_2$ Control (continuous-time)

Consider the system in state-space representation:

$$ \begin{aligned} & x(t)=A x(t)+B_2 u(t)+B_1 w(t) \\ & y(t)=H x(t)+D u(t) \end{aligned} $$ with $x\in\mathbb{R}^n$, $u\in\mathbb{R}^m$, $y\in\mathbb{R}^p$, $w\in\mathbb{R}^q$.

Optimal $\mathcal{H}_2$ Control (continuous-time)

Problem: find $K \in \mathbb{R}^{m \times n}$ such that $u(t)=K x(t)$ stabilize and minimize the $\mathscr{H}_2$ norm of the closed-loop system:

$$ \begin{aligned} \dot{x}(t) & =\left(A+B_2 K\right) x(t)+B_1 w(t) \\ y(t) & =(H+D K) x(t) \\ G(s) & =(H+D K)\left(s I-\left(A+B_2 K\right)\right)^{-1} B_1 \end{aligned} $$

Optimal $\mathcal{H}_2$ Control (continuous-time)

$$ \begin{gathered} \min & \|G\|_2^2 \\ K & \end{gathered} $$ with $$ G(s) =(H+D K)\left(s I-\left(A+B_2 K\right)\right)^{-1} B_1 $$

Obs: LQR with $Q=H^\trp H, R=D^\trp D$ e $S=H^\trp D$ and
$x_0x_0^\trp=B_1B_1^\trp$

Optimal $\mathcal{H}_2$ vs LQR

$$ J=\int_0^\infty \left(x^\trp Q x+u^\trp R u+2x^\trp S u\right) dt $$ with $Q=H^\trp H, R=D^\trp D$ e $S=H^\trp D$

$$ \Rightarrow J=\int_0^\infty \|Hx+Du\|^2_2 dt\\ =\int_0^\infty \|y\|^2_2 dt $$

Optimal $\mathcal{H}_2$ vs LQR

Note that

$$ J=x_0^\trp P x_0. $$

For $x_0x_0^\trp=B_1B_1^\trp$, one has

$$ J=\int_0^\infty \|y\|^2_2 dt = x_0^\trp P x_0 = \text{tr}(B_1^\trp P B_1) $$

Optimal $\mathcal{H}_2$ Control (continuous-time)

$$ \begin{gathered} \min & \|G\|_2^2 \\ K & \end{gathered} $$ with $$ G(s) =(H+D K)\left(s I-\left(A+B_2 K\right)\right)^{-1} B_1 $$

Solve using LMIs!

Optimal $\mathcal{H}_2$ Control (continuous-time)

Define $$ \tilde{A}=A+B_2 K, \quad \tilde{H}=H+D K $$ Thus, the $\mathcal{H}_2$ control problem can be written as:

$$ \begin{gathered} \min & \tr[B_1^\trp PB_1] \\ K,P & \\ \text{s. t.} & \tilde{A}^\trp P+P \tilde{A}+ \tilde{H}^\trp \tilde{H} \preceq 0 \end{gathered} $$

Optimal $\mathcal{H}_2$ Control (continuous-time)

Therefore, $$ (A+B_2 K)^\trp P+P (A+B_2 K)+ (H+D K)^\trp (H+D K) \preceq 0 $$ which is not a LMI! However, using Schur's complement we can rewrite it as $$ \begin{bmatrix} (A+B_2 K)^\trp P+P (A+B_2 K) & (H+D K)^\trp \\ (H+D K) & -I \end{bmatrix} \preceq 0 $$

Optimal $\mathcal{H}_2$ Control (continuous-time)

In addition, we can apply a congruence transformation to the matrix inequality, $$ \small \begin{bmatrix} P^{-1} & 0 \\ 0 & I \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} (A+B_2 K)^\trp P+P (A+B_2 K) & (H+D K)^\trp \\ (H+D K) & -I \end{bmatrix} \Bigr ( \star \Bigl) \preceq 0 $$ which leads to the following $$ \small \begin{bmatrix} P^{-1}A^\trp +AP^{-1}+P^{-1}K^\trp B_2^\trp+B_2 K P^{-1} & P^{-1}H^\trp+ P^{-1}K^\trp D^\trp \\ HP^{-1}+D KP^{-1} & -I \end{bmatrix} \preceq 0 $$

Optimal $\mathcal{H}_2$ Control (continuous-time)

Now, introduce the change of variables $Z=KP^{-1}$ and $W=P^{-1}$, we then can rewrite:

$$ \small \Rightarrow \begin{bmatrix} WA^\trp +AW+Z^\trp B_2^\trp+B_2 Z & WH^\trp+ Z^\trp D^\trp \\ HW+DZ & -I \end{bmatrix} \preceq 0 $$

Now it is a LMI in the variables $W, Z$ ! and $$ K=Z W^{-1} $$

Optimal $\mathcal{H}_2$ Control (continuous-time)

Accordingly, the optimal $\mathcal{H}_2$ control problem can be formulated as:

$$ \small \begin{gathered} \min & \tr[B_1^\trp W^{-1}B_1] \\ W,Z & \\ \text{s. t.} & \begin{bmatrix} WA^\trp +AW+Z^\trp B_2^\trp+B_2 Z & WH^\trp+ Z^\trp D^\trp \\ HW+DZ & -I \end{bmatrix} \preceq 0 \end{gathered} $$

Is this a convex optimization problem?

Optimal $\mathcal{H}_2$ Control (continuous-time)

Note that,

$$ \small \begin{gathered} \min & \tr[X] \\ W,Z,X & \\ \text{s. t.} & \begin{bmatrix} WA^\trp +AW+Z^\trp B_2^\trp+B_2 Z & WH^\trp+ Z^\trp D^\trp \\ HW+DZ & -I \end{bmatrix} \preceq 0\\ & X\succeq B_1^\trp W^{-1} B_1 \end{gathered} $$

Is this a convex optimization problem?

Optimal $\mathcal{H}_2$ Control (continuous-time)

Note that,

$$ \small \begin{gathered} \min & \tr[X] \\ W,Z,X & \\ \text{s. t.} & \begin{bmatrix} WA^\trp +AW+Z^\trp B_2^\trp+B_2 Z & WH^\trp+ Z^\trp D^\trp \\ HW+DZ & -I \end{bmatrix} \preceq 0\\ & \begin{bmatrix} X & B_1^\trp \\ B_1 & W \end{bmatrix}\succeq 0 \end{gathered} $$

Optimal $\mathcal{H}_2$ Control (continuous-time)

$$ \begin{gathered} \min \tr[X]\\ X=X^\trp, Z, W=W^\trp>0 \\ {\left[\begin{array}{cc} X & B_1^{\trp} \\ B_1 & W \end{array}\right]\succeq 0} \\ {\left[\begin{array}{cc} A W+W A^{\trp}+Z^{\trp}B_2^{\trp} + B_2 Z & W H^{\trp}+Z^{\trp} D^{\trp} \\ H W+D Z & -I \end{array}\right]\preceq 0} \end{gathered} $$ and $\|G\|_2^2=\tr[X], K=Z W^{-1}$.

Robust $\mathcal{H}_2$ Control (continuous-time)

$$ \small \begin{gathered} \min \tr[X]\\ X=X^\trp, Z, W=W^\trp>0 \\ {\left[\begin{array}{cc} X & B_1^{\trp}(\alpha) \\ \star & W \end{array}\right]\succeq 0} \\ {\left[\begin{array}{cc} A(\alpha) W+ B_2(\alpha) Z + (\star)^\trp & W H^{\trp}(\alpha)+Z^{\trp} D(\alpha)^{\trp} \\ \star & -I \end{array}\right]\preceq 0} \end{gathered} $$ and $\|G\|_2^2=\tr[X], K=Z W^{-1}$.

Robust $\mathcal{H}_2$ Control (continuous-time)

$$ \small \begin{gathered} \min \tr[X]\\ X=X^\trp, Z, W=W^\trp>0 \\ {\left[\begin{array}{cc} X & B_{1,i}^{\trp} \\ \star & W \end{array}\right]\succeq 0} \\ {\left[\begin{array}{cc} A_i W+ B_{2,i} Z + (\star)^\trp & W H_i^{\trp}+Z^{\trp} D_i^{\trp} \\ \star & -I \end{array}\right]\preceq 0} \end{gathered} $$ for all $i=1,\ldots,N$ and $\|G\|_2^2\le \tr[X], K=Z W^{-1}$.
