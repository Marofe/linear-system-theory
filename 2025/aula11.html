Continous-time State-space Model

 

$$ \begin{aligned} \dot{x}&=Ax+Bw\\ y&=Cx+Dw \end{aligned} $$ with $x\in\mathbb{R}^n$, $w\in\mathbb{R}^m$, $y\in\mathbb{R}^p$.

with $w$ as the disturbance (or noise) input and $y$ as the output.

MIMO Transfer Function

$$ G(s)=C(sI-A)^{-1}B+D=\begin{bmatrix} G_{11}(s) & \cdots & G_{1m}(s)\\ G_{21}(s) & \cdots & G_{2m}(s)\\ \vdots & \ddots & \vdots\\ G_{p1}(s) & \cdots & G_{pm}(s) \end{bmatrix} $$

such that $G_{ij}(s)$ is the transfer function from the $j$-th input to the $i$-th output.

$\mathcal{H}_\infty$ norm (Frequency domain)

$$ \|G\|_\infty=\max_{\omega\in\mathbb{R}} \sigma_\text{max}(G(j\omega)) $$

where $\sigma_\text{max}(G(j\omega))$ is the maximum singular value of the transfer function $G(j\omega)$.
Obs: $$ \sigma_i(G(j\omega))=\sqrt{\lambda_i(G(j\omega)^*G(j\omega))} $$

$\mathcal{H}_\infty$ norm (Frequency domain)

 

For SISO systems, the $\mathcal{H}_\infty$ norm is given by:

$$ \|G\|_\infty=\max_{\omega\in\mathbb{R}} |G(j\omega)| $$

It is the maximum gain of the system over all frequencies.

$\mathcal{H}_\infty$ norm (Time domain)

Suppose that the input $w$ is $\mathcal{L}_2$, i.e., $$ \|w\|_2^2=\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} w(t)^\trp w(t)dt<\infty $$ Then, the $\mathcal{H}_\infty$ norm is defined as the minimum value $\gamma$ such that

$$ \|y(t)\|_2< \gamma \|w(t)\|_2 $$

$\mathcal{H}_\infty$ norm (Time domain)

Suppose that the input $w$ is $\mathcal{L}_2$, i.e., $$ \|w\|_2^2=\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} w(t)^\trp w(t)dt<\infty $$ Then, the $\mathcal{H}_\infty$ norm is defined as the minimum value $\gamma$ such that

$$ \|y(t)\|_2^2< \gamma^2 \|w(t)\|_2^2 $$

$\mathcal{H}_\infty$ norm (Time domain)

Suppose that the input $w$ is $\mathcal{L}_2$, i.e., $$ \|w\|_2^2=\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} w(t)^\trp w(t)dt<\infty $$ Then, the $\mathcal{H}_\infty$ norm is defined as the minimum value $\gamma$ such that

$$ \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} y(t)^\trp y(t) dt < \gamma^2 \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} w(t)^\trp w(t) dt $$

$\mathcal{H}_\infty$ norm (Time domain)

 

Definition of the $\mathcal{H}_\infty$ norm in the time domain:

$$ \|G\|_\infty < \gamma \\ \Leftrightarrow \\ \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} y(t)^\trp y(t) dt < \gamma^2 \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} w(t)^\trp w(t) dt $$

$\mathcal{H}_\infty$ norm and Stability

 

Can a Unstable System have a finite $\mathcal{H}_\infty$ norm?

$$ \|G\|_\infty< \gamma \\ \Leftrightarrow \\ \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} y(t)^\trp y(t) dt < \gamma^2 \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} w(t)^\trp w(t) dt $$

$\mathcal{H}_\infty$ norm and Stability

 

Consider the Lyapunov function $V(x)=x^\trp P x$ with $P>0$.
In addition, require that

$$ \dot{V}+y(t)^\trp y(t)-\gamma^2 w(t)^\trp w(t) < 0 $$

$\mathcal{H}_\infty$ norm and Stability

 

$$ \left\begin{array}{c} x \\ w \end{array}\right]^{\trp}\left\begin{array}{cc} A^{\trp} P+P A+C^{\trp} C & P B+C^{\trp} D \\ B^{\trp} P+D^{\trp} C & D^{\trp} D-\gamma^2 \mathbf{I} \end{array}\right]\left\begin{array}{c} x \\ w \end{array}\right]<0 $$ Thus, $$ \Leftrightarrow \left\begin{array}{cc} A^{\trp} P+P A+C^{\trp} C & P B+C^{\trp} D \\ B^{\trp} P+D^{\trp} C & D^{\trp} D-\gamma^2 \mathbf{I} \end{array}\right]\prec 0 $$

$\mathcal{H}_\infty$ norm (Bounded Real Lemma)

A system is stable and has a finite $\mathcal{H}_\infty$ norm if and only if there exists a positive definite matrix $P$ such that:

$$ \Leftrightarrow \left[\begin{array}{cc} A^{\trp} P+P A+C^{\trp} C & P B+C^{\trp} D \\ B^{\trp} P+D^{\trp} C & D^{\trp} D-\gamma^2 \mathbf{I} \end{array}\right]\prec 0 $$

and $$ \|G\|_\infty< \gamma $$

$\mathcal{H}_\infty$ norm via LMI

Define $\mu=\gamma^2$ and consider the following LMI:

$$ \begin{align*} & \quad\quad\quad\quad \quad\quad \min_{\mu,P=P^\trp \succ 0} \mu\\ \text{s.t.} & \left[\begin{array}{cc} A^{\trp} P+P A+C^{\trp} C & P B+C^{\trp} D \\ B^{\trp} P+D^{\trp} C & D^{\trp} D-\mu \mathbf{I} \end{array}\right]\prec 0 \end{align*} $$

and $$ \|G\|_\infty= \sqrt{\mu} $$

Discrete-time State-space Model

 

$$ \begin{aligned} x_{k+1}&=Ax_k+Bw_k\\ y_k&=Cx_k+Dw_k \end{aligned} $$ with $x\in\mathbb{R}^n$, $w\in\mathbb{R}^m$, $y\in\mathbb{R}^p$.

with $w$ as the disturbance (or noise) input and $y$ as the output.

MIMO Transfer Function

$$ G(z)=C(zI-A)^{-1}B+D=\begin{bmatrix} G_{11}(z) & \cdots & G_{1m}(z)\\ G_{21}(z) & \cdots & G_{2m}(z)\\ \vdots & \ddots & \vdots\\ G_{p1}(z) & \cdots & G_{pm}(z) \end{bmatrix} $$

such that $G_{ij}(z)$ is the transfer function from the $j$-th input to the $i$-th output.

$\mathcal{H}_\infty$ norm (Frequency domain)

$$ \|G\|_\infty=\max_{\omega\in[-\pi,\pi]} \sigma_\text{max}(G(e^{j\omega})) $$

where $\sigma_\text{max}(G(e^{j\omega}))$ is the maximum singular value of the transfer function $G(e^{j\omega})$.
Obs: $$ \sigma_i(G(e^{j\omega}))=\sqrt{\lambda_i(G(e^{j\omega})^*G(e^{j\omega}))} $$

$\mathcal{H}_\infty$ norm (Frequency domain)

 

For SISO systems, the $\mathcal{H}_\infty$ norm is given by:

$$ \|G\|_\infty=\max_{\omega\in[-\pi,\pi]} |G(e^{j\omega})| $$

It is the maximum gain of the system over all frequencies.

Suppose that the input $w$ is $\mathcal{L}_2$, i.e., $$ \|w\|_2^2=\sum_{k=-\infty}^{\infty} w_k^\trp w_k<\infty $$ Then, the $\mathcal{H}_\infty$ norm is defined as the minimum value $\gamma$ such that

$$ \|y_k\|_2< \gamma \|w_k\|_2 $$

$\mathcal{H}_\infty$ norm (Time domain)

Suppose that the input $w$ is $\mathcal{L}_2$, i.e., $$ \|w\|_2^2=\sum_{k=-\infty}^{\infty} w_k^\trp w_k<\infty $$ Then, the $\mathcal{H}_\infty$ norm is defined as the minimum value $\gamma$ such that

$$ \|y_k\|_2^2< \gamma^2 \|w_k\|_2^2 $$

$\mathcal{H}_\infty$ norm (Time domain)

Suppose that the input $w$ is $\mathcal{L}_2$, i.e., $$ \|w\|_2^2=\sum_{k=-\infty}^{\infty} w_k^\trp w_k<\infty $$ Then, the $\mathcal{H}_\infty$ norm is defined as the minimum value $\gamma$ such that

$$ \sum_{k=-\infty}^{\infty} y_k^\trp y_k < \gamma^2 \sum_{k=-\infty}^{\infty} w_k^\trp w_k $$

$\mathcal{H}_\infty$ norm (Time domain)

 

Definition of the $\mathcal{H}_\infty$ norm in the time domain:

$$ \|G\|_\infty< \gamma \\ \Leftrightarrow \\ \sum_{k=-\infty}^{\infty} y_k^\trp y_k < \gamma^2 \sum_{k=-\infty}^{\infty} w_k^\trp w_k $$

$\mathcal{H}_\infty$ norm and Stability

 

Can a Unstable System have a finite $\mathcal{H}_\infty$ norm?

$$ \|G\|_\infty< \gamma \\ \Leftrightarrow \\ \sum_{k=-\infty}^{\infty} y_k^\trp y_k < \gamma^2 \sum_{k=-\infty}^{\infty} w_k^\trp w_k $$

$\mathcal{H}_\infty$ norm and Stability

 

Consider the Lyapunov function $V_k(x_k)=x_k^\trp P x_k$ with $P>0$.
In addition, require that

$$ V_{k+1}(x_{k+1})-V_k(x_k)+y_k^\trp y_k-\gamma^2 w_k^\trp w_k < 0 $$

$\mathcal{H}_\infty$ norm and Stability

 

$$ \small \left[\begin{array}{c} x_k \\ w_k \end{array}\right]^{\trp}\left[\begin{array}{cc} A^{\trp} P A -P+C^{\trp} C & A^\trp P B+C^{\trp} D \\ B^{\trp} P A+D^{\trp} C & B^\trp P B+D^{\trp} D-\gamma^2 \mathbf{I} \end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{c} x_k \\ w_k \end{array}\right]<0 $$ Thus, $$ \Leftrightarrow \left[\begin{array}{cc} A^{\trp} P A -P+C^{\trp} C & A^\trp P B+C^{\trp} D \\ B^{\trp} P A+D^{\trp} C & B^\trp P B+D^{\trp} D-\gamma^2 \mathbf{I} \end{array}\right]\prec 0 $$

$\mathcal{H}_\infty$ norm (Bounded Real Lemma)

A system is stable and has a finite $\mathcal{H}_\infty$ norm if and only if there exists a positive definite matrix $P$ such that:

$$ \Leftrightarrow \left[\begin{array}{cc} A^{\trp} P A -P+C^{\trp} C & A^\trp P B+C^{\trp} D \\ B^{\trp} P A+D^{\trp} C & B^\trp P B+D^{\trp} D-\gamma^2 \mathbf{I} \end{array}\right]\prec 0 $$

and $$ \|G\|_\infty< \gamma $$

$\mathcal{H}_\infty$ norm via LMI

Define $\mu=\gamma^2$ and consider the following LMI:

$$ \begin{align*} & \quad\quad \quad\quad\quad \quad\quad \min_{\mu,P=P^\trp \succ 0} \mu\\ \text{s.t.} & \left[\begin{array}{cc} A^{\trp} P A -P+C^{\trp} C & A^\trp P B+C^{\trp} D \\ B^{\trp} P A+D^{\trp} C & B^\trp P B+D^{\trp} D-\mu \mathbf{I} \end{array}\right]\prec 0 \end{align*} $$

and $$ \|G\|_\infty= \sqrt{\mu} $$

Continous-time State-space Model

 

$$ \begin{aligned} \dot{x}&=Ax+B_2u+B_1w\\ y&=Cx+Dw \end{aligned} $$ with $x\in\mathbb{R}^n$, $u\in\mathbb{R}^m$, $w\in\mathbb{R}^m$, $y\in\mathbb{R}^p$.

with $w$ as the disturbance (or noise) input and $y$ as the output.

Continous-time State-space Model

Find $K\in\mathbb{R}^{m\times p}$ such that the closed-loop system

$$ \begin{aligned} \dot{x}&=(A+B_2K)x+B_1w\\ y&=(C+DK)x\\ G(s)&=(C+DK)(sI-(A+B_2K))^{-1}B_1 \end{aligned} $$

is stable and minimizes the $\mathcal{H}_\infty$ norm.

Continous-time State-space Model

Let $$ \begin{aligned} \tilde{A}&=(A+B_2K),\\ \tilde{C}&=(C+DK). \end{aligned} $$ Thus, $\|G\|_\infty <\gamma $ if and only if there exists a positive definite matrix $P$ such that:

$$ \left[\begin{array}{cc} \tilde{A}^{\trp} P+P \tilde{A}+\tilde{C}^{\trp} \tilde{C} & P B_1 \\ B_1^{\trp} P & -\gamma^2 \mathbf{I} \end{array}\right]\prec 0 $$

Continous-time State-space Model

Note that

$$ \left[\begin{array}{cc} \tilde{A}^{\trp} P+P \tilde{A}+\tilde{C}^{\trp} \tilde{C} & P B_1 \\ B_1^{\trp} P & -\gamma^2 \mathbf{I} \end{array}\right]\prec 0 \\ \Leftrightarrow \left[\begin{array}{cc} \tilde{A}^{\trp} P+P \tilde{A} & P B_1 \\ B_1^{\trp} P & -\gamma^2 \mathbf{I} \end{array}\right]-\left[\begin{array}{cc} \tilde{C}^\trp \\ 0 \end{array}\right](-\mathbf{I})\left[\begin{array}{cc} \tilde{C} & 0 \end{array}\right]\prec 0\\ \Leftrightarrow \left[\begin{array}{cc} \tilde{A}^{\trp} P+P \tilde{A} & P B_1 & \tilde{C}^\trp \\ B_1^{\trp} P & -\gamma^2 \mathbf{I} & 0\\ \tilde{C} & 0 & -I \end{array}\right]\prec 0 $$

Continous-time State-space Model

Apply congruence transformation to the LMI:

$$ \begin{bmatrix} P^{-1} & 0 & 0\\ 0 & I & 0\\ 0 & 0 & I \end{bmatrix} \left[\begin{array}{cc} \tilde{A}^{\trp} P+P \tilde{A} & P B_1 & \tilde{C}^\trp \\ B_1^{\trp} P & -\gamma^2 \mathbf{I} & 0\\ \tilde{C} & 0 & -I \end{array}\right]\begin{bmatrix} P^{-1} & 0 & 0\\ 0 & I & 0\\ 0 & 0 & I \end{bmatrix}\prec 0\\ \Leftrightarrow \left[\begin{array}{cc} P^{-1}\tilde{A}^{\trp}+\tilde{A}P^{-1} & B_1 & P^{-1}\tilde{C}^\trp \\ B_1^{\trp} & -\gamma^2 \mathbf{I} & 0\\ \tilde{C}P^{-1} & 0 & -I \end{array}\right]\prec 0 $$

Continous-time State-space Model

 

Thus,

$$ \small \left[\begin{array}{cc} P^{-1}(A+B_2K)^{\trp}+(A+B_2K)P^{-1} & B_1 & P^{-1}(C+DK)^\trp \\ \star & -\gamma^2 \mathbf{I} & 0\\ \star & \star & -I \end{array}\right]\prec 0\\ \Rightarrow \left[\begin{array}{cc} P^{-1}A^\trp+P^{-1}K^{\trp}B_2^\trp+(\star)^\trp & B_1 & P^{-1}(C+DK)^\trp \\ \star & -\gamma^2 \mathbf{I} & 0\\ \star & \star & -I \end{array}\right]\prec 0\\ $$

Continous-time $\mathcal{H}_\infty$ Control

 

Let $W=P^{-1}$ and $Z=KW$, then

$$ \left[\begin{array}{cc} WA^\trp+AW+Z^\trp B_2^\trp+B_2Z & B_1 & WC^\trp +Z^\trp D^\trp \\ \star & -\gamma^2 \mathbf{I} & 0\\ \star & \star & -I \end{array}\right]\prec 0\\ $$

Continous-time $\mathcal{H}_\infty$ Optimal Control

$$\small \begin{align*} & \quad\quad\quad\quad\quad \quad\quad \min_{\mu,Z,W=W^\trp \succ 0} \mu\\ \text{s.t.} & \left[\begin{array}{cc} WA^\trp+AW+Z^\trp B_2^\trp+B_2Z & B_1 & WC^\trp +Z^\trp D^\trp \\ \star & -\mu \mathbf{I} & 0\\ \star & \star & -I \end{array}\right]\prec 0\\ \end{align*} $$ and $K=ZW^{-1}$.
In addition, the $\mathcal{H}_\infty$ norm satisfies $\|G\|_\infty= \sqrt{\mu}$.

Continous-time Polytopic State-space Model

 

$$ \begin{aligned} \dot{x}&=A(\alpha)x+B_2(\alpha)u+B_1(\alpha)w\\ y&=C(\alpha)x+D(\alpha)w \end{aligned} $$ with $x\in\mathbb{R}^n$, $u\in\mathbb{R}^m$, $w\in\mathbb{R}^m$, $y\in\mathbb{R}^p$.

with $w$ as the disturbance (or noise) input and $y$ as the output.

Continous-time Robust $\mathcal{H}_\infty$ Control

$$\small \begin{align*} & \quad\quad\quad\quad\quad \quad\quad \min_{\mu,Z,W=W^\trp \succ 0} \mu\\ \text{s.t.} & \left[\begin{array}{cc} WA_i^\trp+Z^\trp B_{2,i}^\trp+(\star)^\trp & B_{1,i} & WC_i^\trp +Z^\trp D_i^\trp \\ \star & -\mu \mathbf{I} & 0\\ \star & \star & -I \end{array}\right]\prec 0\\ \end{align*} $$ and $K=ZW^{-1}$.
In addition, the $\mathcal{H}_\infty$ norm satisfies $\|G\|_\infty\le \sqrt{\mu}$.
