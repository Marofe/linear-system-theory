What is stability?

Definition: stability for a dynamic system is the hability to stay near a equilibrium point!

Consider a linear dynamic system in continuous time: $$ \dot{x}=Ax $$ How many equilibrium points there is?

Transformed systems

If the equilibrium point of interest is not at the origin, may define $$w=x-x_e$$

Thus, a linear dynamic system in continuous time is: $$ \dot{w}=Aw $$ will have the origin as equilibrium point!

Stability

Consider a linear homogenous systems in the form

$$ \dot{x}=Ax,\quad x(0)=x_0 $$

The state solution is $$ x(t,x_0)=e^{At}x_0,\quad t\ge 0 $$

Stability

Definition: The equilibrium $x = 0$ is said to be stable if for every $\epsilon > 0$, there exists a $\delta(\epsilon) > 0$ such that $$ \begin{equation} \| x(t, x_0) \| < \epsilon \quad \textit{for all } t \geq 0 \tag{4.9} \end{equation} $$ whenever $$ \begin{equation} \| x_0 \| < \delta(\epsilon). \tag{4.10} \end{equation} $$

Stability

Asymptotic Stability

Definition: The equilibrium $x = 0$ is said to be asymptotically stable if

  • it is stable;
  • there exists an $\eta > 0$ such that $$\lim\limits_{t \to \infty} x(t, x_0) = 0$$ whenever $\| x_0 \| < \eta$.

Asymptotic Stability

Exponential Stability

Definition: The equilibrium $x = 0$ is said to be exponetially stable if there is $\alpha > 0$ and for every $\epsilon >0$, there exist $\delta(\epsilon)>0$ such that $$ \|x(t,x_0)\| \le \epsilon e^{-\alpha t},\quad \forall t\ge 0 $$ whenever $$ \|x_0\|\le \delta(\epsilon) $$

Exponential Stability

Exponential Stability

Consider a state-space system in the form $$ \dot{x}=Ax $$

The state-space system is stable in continuous time if $$ \begin{aligned} \text{real}(\lambda\{A\}) < 0 \end{aligned} $$

We say that the matrix $A$ is Hurwitz!

Exponential Stability

Consider a state-space system in the form $$ x_{k+1}=A_dx_k $$

The state-space system is stable in discrete time if $$ \begin{aligned} |\lambda\{A_d\}| < 1 \end{aligned} $$

We say that the matrix $A_d$ is Schur!

Lyapunov Stability: continuous time

Energy function:

If there exists a function $V:\mathbb{R}^n\to \mathbb{R}$ such that $$ V(x)>0,\quad \forall x\in\mathbb{R}^n \\ V(x)=0 \Leftrightarrow x=0 $$ and $$ \dot{V}(x) \le 0 $$ then the system is stable.

Lyapunov Stability: continuous time

Energy function:

If there exists a function $V:\mathbb{R}^n\to \mathbb{R}$ such that $$ V(x)>0,\quad \forall x\in\mathbb{R}^n \\ V(x)=0 \Leftrightarrow x=0 $$ and $$ \dot{V}(x) < 0 $$ then the system is asymptotically stable to zero.

Asymptotic Stability from Lyapunov Sense

Lyapunov Stability: continuous time

 

Energy function:
For linear systems, the energy function

$$ V(x)=x^\trp P x,\quad P=P^\trp \succ 0 $$

Lyapunov Stability: continuous time

$$ V(x)=x^\trp P x,\quad P=P^\trp \succ 0 $$

Note that

  • $V(x)>0,~\forall x\in\mathbb{R}^n$
  • $V(x)=0 \Leftrightarrow x=0$
  • $\dot{V}(x)\le 0$ or $\dot{V}(x)< 0$ ?

Lyapunov Stability: continuous time

Note that

$ \dot{V}(x)= \dot{x}^\trp P x+x^\trp P\dot{x} $


Since, $ \dot{x}=Ax $ then

$$ (Ax)^\trp P+ x^\trp P(Ax) < 0 $$

Lyapunov Stability: continuous time

Note that

$ \dot{V}(x)= \dot{x}^\trp P x+x^\trp P\dot{x} $


Since, $ \dot{x}=Ax $ then

$$ x^\trp (A^\trp P+ PA)x < 0 $$

Lyapunov Stability: continuous time

 

A continuous-time state-space system is asymptotically stable if and only if there exists a matrix $P=P^\trp \in\mathbb{R}^{n\times n}$ such that

$$ P\succ 0 \\ A^\trp P+PA \prec 0 $$

Linear Matrix Inequality (LMI)

Lyapunov Stability: continuous time

 

A discrete-time state-space system is asymptotically stable if and only if there exists a matrix $P=P^\trp\in\mathbb{R}^{n\times n}$ such that

$$ P\succ 0, \\ A^\trp P+PA = -Q, \quad Q\succ 0 $$

Linear system of equations!

Kronecker product

 

Define the vectorization operator $\text{vec}(A)$ as the vector formed by stacking the columns of $A$ on top of each other.

$$ \text{vec}(ABC)=(C^\trp \otimes A)\text{vec}(B) $$

Lyapunov Stability: continuous time

 

$$ \text{vec}(A^\trp P+PA) = -\text{vec}(Q)\\ $$ Thus,

$$ \Rightarrow \underbrace{(I \otimes A^\trp + A^\trp\otimes I)}_{M}\underbrace{\text{vec}(P)}_{p} = \underbrace{-\text{vec}(Q)}_{q} $$

Lyapunov Stability: continuous time

 

$$ \text{vec}(A^\trp P+PA) = -\text{vec}(Q)\\ $$ Thus,

$$ p=M^{-1}q $$

Lyapunov Stability: discrete time

Energy function:

If there exists a function $V:\mathbb{R}^n\to \mathbb{R}$ such that $$ V(x_k)>0,\quad \forall x_k\in\mathbb{R}^n \\ V(x_k)=0 \Leftrightarrow x_k=0 $$ and $$ V(x_{k+1})-V(x_{k}) \le 0 $$ then the system is stable (BIBO).

Lyapunov Stability: discrete time

Energy function:

If there exists a function $V:\mathbb{R}^n\to \mathbb{R}$ such that $$ V(x_k)>0,\quad \forall x_k\in\mathbb{R}^n \\ V(x_k)=0 \Leftrightarrow x_k=0 $$ and $$ V(x_{k+1})-V(x_{k}) < 0 $$ then the system is asymptotically stable to zero.

Lyapunov Stability: discrete time

 

Energy function:
For linear systems, the energy function

$$ V(x_k)=x_k^\trp P x_k,\quad P=P^\trp \succ 0 $$

Lyapunov Stability: discrete time

Energy function:
$$ V(x_{k+1})-V(x_k)=x_{k+1}^\trp Px_{k+1}-x_k^\trp Px_k < 0 $$ Since, $$ x_{k+1}=A_dx_k $$ then

A discrete-time state-space system is asymptotically stable if and only if there exists a matrix $P=P^\trp \in\mathbb{R}^{n\times n}$ such that

$$ P\succ 0 \\ A_d^\trp PA_d -P \prec 0 $$

Polytopic Matrix $A$

Consider the uncertain matrix $A$ as a linear combination of $m$ matrices $A_i$: $$ A(\alpha)=\alpha_1A_1+\alpha_2A_2+\cdots+\alpha_NA_N $$ where $\alpha_i\in\mathbb{R}^+$ and $\sum_{i=1}^N \alpha_i=1$. Then $A(\alpha)$ is called a polytopic uncertain matrix.

Polytopic System

Dynamic system: $$ \dot{x}=A(\alpha)x $$

Robust Lyapunov Stability: continuous time

A continuous-time polytopic uncertain state-space system is asymptotically stable if and only if

$$ \exists P(\alpha)=P(\alpha)^\trp \succ 0 \\ A(\alpha)^\trp P(\alpha)+P(\alpha)A(\alpha) \prec 0\\ \forall \alpha \in \Lambda_N=\{\alpha \in \mathbb{R}^N:\sum_i\alpha_i=1 \text{ and } \alpha_i\ge 0\} $$

Parameter-dependent LMI!
(infinite dimensional problem)

Quadratic Stability: continuous time

A continuous-time polytopic uncertain state-space system is asymptotically stable if

$$ \exists P=P^\trp \succ 0 \\ A(\alpha)^\trp P+PA(\alpha) \prec 0\\ \forall \alpha \in \Lambda_N=\{\alpha \in \mathbb{R}^N:\sum_i\alpha_i=1 \text{ and } \alpha_i\ge 0\} $$

Quadratic Stability: continuous time

A continuous-time polytopic uncertain state-space system is asymptotically stable if

$$ \exists P=P^\trp \succ 0 \\ A_1^\trp P+PA_1 \prec 0\\ A_2^\trp P+PA_2 \prec 0\\ \vdots \\ A_N^\trp P+PA_N \prec 0\\ $$

LMI!

Quadratic Stability: discrete time

A discrete-time polytopic uncertain state-space system is asymptotically stable if

$$ \exists P=P^\trp \succ 0 \\ A_1^\trp PA_1-P \prec 0\\ A_2^\trp PA_2-P \prec 0\\ \vdots \\ A_N^\trp PA_N-P \prec 0\\ $$

LMI!

Polytopic Time-Varying Systems

$$ \dot{x}=A(\alpha(t))x $$

Stability of Time-Varying Systems

Consider the uncertain matrix $A$ as a linear combination of $N$ matrices $A_i$ that changes with time:

$$ \dot{x}=A(\alpha(t))x $$

The system is asymptotically stable if

$$ \exists P=P^\trp \succ 0 \\ A_i^\trp P+PA_i \prec 0\\ \forall i=1,\ldots, N $$
