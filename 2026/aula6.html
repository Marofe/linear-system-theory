State-Space Model

System: $$ \begin{aligned} x_{k+1}&=A_dx_k+B_du_k\\ y_k&=Hx_k+Du_k \end{aligned} $$

Observer: $$ \begin{aligned} \hat{x}_{k+1}&=A_d\hat{x}_k+B_du_k+L(y_k-\hat{y}_k)\\ \hat{y}_k&=H\hat{x}_k+Du_k \end{aligned} $$

State Error Dynamics

$$ \begin{aligned} \varepsilon_{k+1}&=x_{k+1}-\hat{x}_{k+1}\\ &=Ax_k+Bu_k-(A\hat{x}_k+Bu_k)+L(y_k-\hat{y}_k)\\ &=A\varepsilon_{k}+L(Hx_k+Du_k-(H\hat{x}_k+Du_k))\\ &=(A-LH)\varepsilon_k \end{aligned} $$

Therefore,

$$ \varepsilon_{k+1}=(A-LH)\varepsilon_k $$

State Error Dynamics

Hence, if

$$ |\lambda\{A-LH\}| < 1 $$

then the observer converges to the true state!

 

Good design practice: the state error dynamics of the observer should be at least 2x faster than the system dynamics!

Ackermann's Equation (SISO)

Given the desired characteristic equation for the observer: $$ \gamma(z)=z^{n}+\beta_1z^{n-1}+\cdots+\beta_{n-1}z+\beta_n $$ then, the observer gain is given by:

$$ L=\gamma(A)\mathcal{O}^{-1}\begin{bmatrix} 0 & 0 & \cdots & 1 \end{bmatrix}^\trp $$

note: $\gamma(A)=A^{n}+\beta_1A^{n-1}+\cdots+\beta_{n-1}A+\beta_n I$

State-Space Model

System: $$ \begin{aligned} \dot{x}&=Ax+Bu\\ y&=Hx+Du \end{aligned} $$

Observer: $$ \begin{aligned} \dot{\hat{x}}&=A\hat{x}+Bu+L(y-\hat{y})\\ \hat{y}&=H\hat{x}+Du \end{aligned} $$

State Error Dynamics

$$ \begin{aligned} \dot{\varepsilon}&=\dot{x}-\dot{\hat{x}}\\ &=Ax+Bu-(A\hat{x}+Bu)+L(y-\hat{y})\\ &=A\varepsilon+L(Hx+Du-(H\hat{x}+Du))\\ &=(A-LH)\varepsilon \end{aligned} $$

Therefore,

$$ \dot{\varepsilon}=(A-LH)\varepsilon $$

State Error Dynamics

Hence, if

$$ \mathcal{R}\{\lambda\{A-LH\}\} < 0 $$

then the observer converges to the true state!

 

Good design practice: the state error dynamics of the observer should be at least 2x faster than the system dynamics!

Stochastic model $$ \begin{aligned} x_{k+1}&=Ax_k+Bu_k+w_k\\ y_k&=Hx_k+Du_k+\epsilon_k \end{aligned} $$

  • $w_k \to $ process noise
  • $\epsilon_k \to $ measurement noise

Stochastic Optimal State Observer
= Kalman Filter

Cost function (MSE):

$$ J=\sum_{k=0}^\infty \mathbb{E}\{\|x_k-\hat{x}_{k}\|^2\} $$

Thus, $$ L^*=\arg\min_L J $$

Gaussian Noise

$$ w_k\sim\mathcal{N}(0,Q),\quad \epsilon_k\sim\mathcal{N}(0,R),\quad i.i.d. $$

Note: for $x\in\mathbb{R}$:
$$ \mathcal{N}(\mu,\sigma^2)=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}\sigma}\exp\Bigl(-\frac{(x-\mu)^2}{2\sigma^2}\Bigr) $$
  • $\mu \to $ mean
  • $\sigma^2 \to $ variance

Multidimensional Gaussian Noise

$$ w_k\sim\mathcal{N}(0,Q),\quad \epsilon_k\sim\mathcal{N}(0,R),\quad i.i.d. $$

Note: for $x\in\mathbb{R}^n$: $$ \mathcal{N}(\mu,\Sigma)=\frac{1}{\sqrt{(2\pi)^n \det{\Sigma}}}\exp(-\frac{1}{2}\|x-\mu\|^2_{\Sigma^{-1}}) $$
  • $\mu \to $ mean
  • $\Sigma \to $ covariance

Multidimensional Gaussian

$$ \mathcal{N}(\mu,\Sigma)=\frac{1}{\sqrt{(2\pi)^n \det{\Sigma}}}\exp(-\frac{1}{2}\|x-\mu\|^2_{\Sigma^{-1}}) $$

Notation for weighted norm: $$ \|x-\mu\|^2_{\Sigma^{-1}}=(x-\mu)^\trp \Sigma^{-1}(x-\mu) $$ and $$ \det(\Sigma) \to \text{matrix determinant operator} $$

Multidimensional Gaussian

$$ \mathcal{N}(\mu,\Sigma)=\frac{1}{\sqrt{(2\pi)^n \det{\Sigma}}}\exp(-\frac{1}{2}\|x-\mu\|^2_{\Sigma^{-1}}) $$

Example for $\mathbb{R}^2$: $$ \mu=\begin{bmatrix}\mu_1\\ \mu_2\end{bmatrix},\quad \Sigma=\begin{bmatrix} \sigma_1^2 & \sigma_{12}\\ \sigma_{21} & \sigma_2^2 \end{bmatrix} $$ note: $\Sigma=\Sigma^\trp >0$

Multidimensional Gaussian

Mean and Covariance

Let $x\sim\mathcal{N}(\mu,\Sigma)$ be a Gaussian random vector, then

Mean: $$ \mathbb{E}\{x\}=\int xp(x)dx = \mu $$

Covariance: $$ \mathbb{E}\{(x-\mu)(x-\mu)^\trp \}=\int (x-\mu)(x-\mu)^\trp p(x)dx = \Sigma $$

Kalman Filter


Rudolf Emil Kalman (1930-2016)

Kalman Filter

Paper in 1960

Kalman Filter

Performance criterion: Minimum Mean Squared Error $$ \min_{\{\hat{x}_k\}_{k=1}^N} \sum_{k=1}^N \mathbb{E}\{\|x_k-\hat{x}_k\|^2\} $$

The optimal solution for linear dynamics with Gaussian noise is the Kalman filter!

The Kalman Filter (Observer)

 

$$ \begin{aligned} \hat{x}_{k+1}=A\hat{x}_{k}+Bu_k+K(y_{k}-H\hat{x}_{k}) \end{aligned} $$

Thus, $$ e_{k+1}=Ax_{k}+Bu_k+w_k-(A\hat{x}_{k}+Bu_k+K(Hx_{k}+\epsilon_{k}-H\hat{x}_{k}))\\ =(A-KH)e_{k}-K\epsilon_{k}+w_k $$

The Kalman Filter (Observer)

 

$$ \begin{aligned} J_{k+1}&=\mathbb{E}[\|e_{k+1}\|_2^2] \\&= \mathbb{E}[e_{k}^\trp (A-KH)^\trp (A-KH)e_{k}+\epsilon_{k}^\trp K^\trp K \epsilon_{k}+w_k^\trp w_k]\\ &=\text{tr}[(A-KH)P_{k} (A-KH)^\trp+KRK^\trp+Q ] \end{aligned} $$

with $$ P_{k}=\mathbb{E}[e_{k}e_{k}^\trp],\quad \mathbb{E}[e_{k}^\trp \epsilon_{k}]=\mathbb{E}[e_{k}^\trp w_{k}]=\mathbb{E}[\epsilon_{k}^\trp w_{k}]=0 $$

The Kalman Filter (Observer)

 

$$ \begin{aligned} J=\sum_{k=0}^\infty J_k \end{aligned} $$

Such that $$ J_{k+1} = \text{tr}[(A-KH)P_{k} (A-KH)^\trp+KRK^\trp+Q ]\ge 0 $$

The Kalman Filter (Observer)

 

$$ \begin{aligned} \min_K J \Leftrightarrow \min_K J_k \quad \forall k \end{aligned} $$

Therefore, $$ K^*=\argmin_K \text{tr}[(A-KH)P_{k} (A-KH)^\trp+KRK^\trp+Q ] $$

The Kalman Filter (Observer)

$$ \begin{aligned} \min_K \text{tr}[(A-KH)P_{k} (A-KH)^\trp+KRK^\trp+Q ] \end{aligned} $$

Trace derivatives properties: $$ \frac{\partial }{\partial X}\tr[XA]=A^\trp,\\ \frac{\partial }{\partial X}\tr[AX^\trp]=A,\\ \frac{\partial }{\partial X}\tr[XAX^\trp]=X(A+A^\trp)\\ $$

The Kalman Filter (Observer)

$$ \begin{aligned} \min_K \text{tr}[-KHP_{k}A^\trp-(\star)^\trp +K(R+HP_{k}H^\trp)K^\trp] \end{aligned} $$

Trace derivatives properties: $$ \frac{\partial }{\partial X}\tr[XA]=A^\trp,\\ \frac{\partial }{\partial X}\tr[AX^\trp]=A,\\ \frac{\partial }{\partial X}\tr[XAX^\trp]=X(A+A^\trp)\\ $$

The Kalman Filter (Observer)

$$ \begin{aligned} \frac{\partial}{\partial K}\text{tr}[-KHP_{k}-(\star)^\trp +K(R+HP_{k}H^\trp)K^\trp]=0 \end{aligned} $$

Thus, $$ \frac{\partial}{\partial K}\text{tr}[KHP_{k}A^\trp]=AP_{k}H^\trp\\ \frac{\partial}{\partial K}\text{tr}[AP_{k}H^\trp K^\trp ]=AP_{k}H^\trp\\ \frac{\partial}{\partial K}\text{tr}[K(R+HP_{k}H^\trp)K^\trp]=2K(R+HP_{k}H^\trp)\\ $$

The Kalman Filter (Observer)

$$ \begin{aligned} -2AP_{k}H^\trp+2K(R+HP_{k}H^\trp)=0 \end{aligned} $$

Consequently, the optimal gain is

$$ K^*=AP_{k}H^\trp(R+HP_{k}H^\trp)^{-1} $$

The Kalman Filter (Observer)

$$ \begin{aligned} J_{k+1}^*=\text{tr}[(A-K^*H)P_{k} (A-K^*H)^\trp+K^*R(K^*)^\trp+Q ] \end{aligned} $$

where

$$ K^*=AP_{k}H^\trp(R+HP_{k}H^\trp)^{-1} $$

But, $$ J_{k+1}^*=\tr[P_{k+1}] $$

The Kalman Filter (Observer)

$$ \begin{aligned} P_{k+1}=(A-K^*H)P_{k} (A-K^*H)^\trp+K^*R(K^*)^\trp+Q \end{aligned} $$

where

$$ K^*=AP_{k}H^\trp(R+HP_{k}H^\trp)^{-1} $$

The Kalman Filter (Observer)

$$ \begin{aligned} P_{k+1}=AP_kA^\trp-K^*HP_{k}A^\trp-(\star)^\trp\\+K^*(R+HP_kH^\trp)(K^*)^\trp+Q \end{aligned} $$

where

$$ K^*=AP_{k}H^\trp(R+HP_{k}H^\trp)^{-1} $$

The Kalman Filter (Observer)

$$ \begin{aligned} P_{k+1}=AP_kA^\trp-AP_kH^\trp(R+HP_kH^\trp)^{-1}HP_kA+Q \end{aligned} $$

Take $k\to\infty$ and we got

$$ \begin{aligned} P_{\infty}=AP_\infty A^\trp-AP_\infty H^\trp(R+HP_\infty H^\trp)^{-1}HP_\infty A+Q \end{aligned} $$

LQR vs Kalman Filter Duality

Kalman Filter:
$$ P_\infty=AP_{\infty} A^\trp - AP_{\infty} H^\trp(R+HP_{\infty} H^\trp )^{-1}HP_{\infty} A^\trp + Q\\ $$

LQR:
$$P_\infty=A^\trp P_{\infty} A - A^\trp P_{\infty} B (R+B^\trp P_{\infty} B )^{-1}B^\trp P_{\infty} A + Q$$

$$ A\to A^\trp, \quad H^\trp \to B $$

LQR vs Kalman Filter Duality

Kalman Filter:
$$ L=AP_\infty H^\trp (R+HP_\infty H^\trp )^{-1} $$

LQR:
$$K^\trp =A^\trp P_\infty B(R+B^\trp P_\infty B )^{-1}$$

$$ A\to A^\trp, \quad H^\trp \to B $$

Lyapunov Stability: continuous time

 

An state observer is asymptotically stable if and only if there exists a matrix $P=P^\trp \in\mathbb{R}^{n\times n}$ and $L\in\mathbb{R}^{m\times n}$ such that

$$ P\succ 0 \\ (A-LH)^\trp P+P(A-LH) \prec 0 $$

This is a LMI?

Lyapunov Stability: continuous time

 

An state observer is asymptotically stable if and only if there exists a matrix $P=P^\trp \in\mathbb{R}^{n\times n}$ and $Z\in\mathbb{R}^{n\times m}$ such that

$$ P\succ 0 \\ A^\trp P+PA-Z^\trp H^\trp -HZ \prec 0 $$

Where $Z=PL$, which implies $L=P^{-1}Z$

Polytopic Uncertain State-Space Model

System: $$ \begin{aligned} \dot{x}&=A(\alpha)x+B(\alpha)u\\ y&=H(\alpha)x+D(\alpha)u \end{aligned} $$

Observer: $$ \begin{aligned} \dot{\hat{x}}&=A(\alpha)\hat{x}+B(\alpha)u+L(y-\hat{y})\\ \hat{y}&=H(\alpha)\hat{x}+D(\alpha)u \end{aligned} $$

State Error Dynamics

$$ \begin{aligned} \dot{\varepsilon}&=\dot{x}-\dot{\hat{x}}\\ &=A(\alpha)x+B(\alpha)u-(A(\alpha)\hat{x}+B(\alpha)u+L(y-\hat{y}))\\ &=A(\alpha)\varepsilon+L(H(\alpha)x+D(\alpha)u-(H(\alpha)\hat{x}+D(\alpha)u))\\ &=(A(\alpha)-LH(\alpha))\varepsilon \end{aligned} $$

Therefore,

$$ \dot{\varepsilon}=(A(\alpha)-LH(\alpha))\varepsilon,\quad \forall \alpha\in\Lambda_N $$

Lyapunov Stability: continuous time

 

An state observer is asymptotically stable if there exists a matrix $P=P^\trp \in\mathbb{R}^{n\times n}$ and $L\in\mathbb{R}^{m\times n}$ such that

$$ P\succ 0 \\ (A(\alpha)-LH(\alpha))^\trp P+P(A(\alpha)-LH(\alpha)) \prec 0\\ \forall \alpha\in\Lambda_N $$

Quadratic Lyapunov Stability: continuous time

An state observer is asymptotically stable if and only if there exists a matrix $P=P^\trp \in\mathbb{R}^{n\times n}$ and $Z\in\mathbb{R}^{n\times m}$ such that

$$ P\succ 0 \\ A_i^\trp P+PA_i-H_i^\trp Z^\trp -Z H_i \prec 0\\ \forall i=1,\ldots,N $$

Where $Z=PL$, which implies $L=P^{-1}Z$
